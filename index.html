Coffe Quality Visualizations ☕

In the present work, we made visualizations about coffee quality using Bokeh. The dataset used was obtained from the Coffee Quality Institute, a reference institution in the evaluation and certification of coffee quality on a global scale. The dataset is avaiable at Coffee Quality Data CQI. The dataset contains data collected in May 2023, and include a wide range of coffee lots that were sent for evaluation.

About

Coffee is a widely appreciated and consumed drink, playing a fundamental role in the culture and economy of various countries. Understanding and assessing coffee quality is of utmost importance for producers, exporters, roasters and baristas.The Coffee Quality Institute plays a crucial role in this context, being responsible for establishing standards and guidelines for the evaluation and classification of coffee quality. Its work is essential to ensure excellence and consistency in the coffee market, promoting the appreciation and recognition of high-quality coffees.

Here we have a graph that plots different coffee varieties against processing methods.

The different processing methods cited refer to methods of removing the many layers of the coffee fruit in order to obtain its seeds. For example, in the most common processing method, washing, many of the layers are removed before the drying stage begins, as opposed to natural processing, in which the fruit is dried before the removal of its layers.

by Larissa

Here we have a graph that plots the amount of kilograms of coffee produced by each country.

By no coincidence, the countries with the highest productions are located in areas of tropical climate, which makes sense, since arabica coffee's optimal temperaturerange is 18℃ - 21℃, tolerating mean annual temperatures up to 24℃.

Above those moderate temperatures, the seeds ripen more quickly. And since coffee beans are the seeds of the plant, that means less coffee quality; exposure to temperatures higher than 30℃ can severely damage the seedlings, stunting growth, yellowing leaves, and even spawning stem tumors.

This plot aims to show the correlation between the coffee's scores on flavor and acidity, using a scatterplot. This type of graphic was chosen because it shows that the dots (representing different coffees) tends to have simillar scores on these aspects, meaning that when one gets higher, the other tends to get higher too. the size of each dot shows how many coffees had tha specific rating, since the scores are given in some cathegorical way, and plotting without that would lead to some dots overlapping others.

The objective of this plot is to show the distributions of altitudes of coffee harvesting for each country. The chosen graphic type is a set of boxplots, since their function is to show the distribution of some variable appearances. With this plot, the viewer is able to see the range of possible altitutes a country harvest their coffees, and also see if a country harvests in a small range of altitudes or if it harvests in varying altitudes.

The objective of this graph is to provide a visual representation of the correlations between coffee scores in sensory variables. By listing the sensory variables on the X and Y axes, we can identify the relationships between them. Each cell in the graph shows the correlation between two specific variables, with the color of the cell indicating the strength of the correlation.

This visual representation allows for a deeper understanding of the interactions between the sensory characteristics of coffee, which directly impact its overall quality.

The relationship between altitude and the flavor of coffee is that, generally, coffee grown at higher altitudes tends to have a more complex and distinctive flavor. This is due to different climatic conditions, such as temperatures and humidity. However, other factors such as coffee variety and cultivation practices also influence the final flavor.

That being said, the objective of the chart below is to demonstrate the relationship between the flavor score of each coffee sample and the altitude at which it was grown.
